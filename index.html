THE ADVENTURE BEGINS

16 APRIL 2016 | FOREST SANCTUARY | SUNSHINE COAST

Welcome to the wedding website of Christopher Kearney and Chantal McNaught. Here you can find the details and RSVP to our special day.

No one likes a standard, boring wedding. Marriage should be the marking of a new adventure! So that’s exactly how we’re setting the scene. The whole day will be steampunk themed.

Christopher Kearney

Groom

Technology cosmonaut Christopher James is an avid cat-lover who enjoys his fermented beverages. Having travelled the vast distances from the township of Rockhampton to the Gold Coast at the age of 18, Christopher found his heart’s keeper in the similarly adventurous and cat-loving Chantal.

Chantal McNaught

Bride

A young and determined adventurer, Chantal always yearns to explore and experience new things in life. Now settled in the enclave dubbed Gold Coast, she uses her power and knowledge of law to grant outsiders with access to Australia. During her travels she found Christopher, someone to share her passions with, and whom to embark with on a new adventure.

“I see that it is by no means useless to travel, if a man wants to see something new”

― Jules Verne, Around the World in Eighty Days

James Hooper

Best Man

This university lecturer, aspiring video games scholar and PhD student is motivated by a sense of adventure and a desire to play. James is ever-willing to accompany his friends.

Anneliese de Vries

Maid of Honour

Anne is an enigma, her motivations and desires in flux with the breeze yet no matter how it blows she carries a flame that burns for adventure.

Robert Rooney

Groomsman

Robbo, Bob, Roberto and many other names Robert goes by are as diverse as he is. A solicitor in Rockhampton, Robert is loyal, honest and as rough as the outback he comes from.

Ellen Bos

Bridesmaid

Determined and headstrong, Ellen shows she proves not all those who wander are lost. Even with this sense of adventure, she is fiercely loyal; few coming between her and who she loves.

“It has long been an axiom of mine that the little things are infinitely the most important.”

– Arthur Conan Doyle, The Memoirs of Sherlock Holmes
